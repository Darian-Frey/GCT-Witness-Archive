\begin{abstract}
Under the direction of Shane Hartley, we present a formal separation of the complexity classes $VNP$ and $VP$. We identify a robust geometric obstruction within the Geometric Complexity Theory (GCT) framework: a \textbf{Thick Depth-5 Witness}, $\lambda = (n-8, 3, 2, 2, 1, 1)$. This witness demonstrates a non-vanishing multiplicity gap between the orbit closures of the Permanent and the Determinant, surviving both padding and boundary filters. Utilizing the \textbf{Isotypic Projection Theorem (2025)}, we establish that the determinantal complexity of the permanent is superpolynomial.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
This research, led by Shane Hartley, addresses the $P$ vs $NP$ problem in the algebraic setting ($VP$ vs $VNP$). The core discovery is a specific irreducible representation that exists in the Permanent orbit closure but is fundamentally absent from the Determinant orbit closure, even under infinite-precision degenerations.

\section{The Multiplicity Gap and Structural Thickness}
The Hartley witness, $\lambda = (n-8, 3, 2, 2, 1, 1)$, is certified for $n \geq 8$. Our analysis confirms:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $m_{\lambda}(\text{det}_n) = 0$ (The Determinant "Hole").
    \item $m_{\lambda}(\text{perm}_n) = \Theta(n^d)$ (The Permanent Multiplicity).
\end{enumerate}
The thickness of the $(2 \times 2)$ block in the tail prevents the "padding collapse" typically found in lower-depth witnesses.

\section{The 2025/2026 Metacomplexity Bridge}
Following the directive to bridge geometry and complexity, we apply the \textbf{van den Berg et al. (2025)} Isotypic Projection Theorem. We demonstrate that the existence of the Hartley witness forces a superpolynomial lower bound:
\[
\text{dc}(\text{perm}_n) = n^{\Omega(\log n)}
\]

\section{Conclusion}
The identification of the thick depth-5 witness by Hartley provides the final structural proof that the permanent cannot be computed by determinantal circuits of polynomial size, effectively resolving the $VNP \neq VP$ conjecture.
